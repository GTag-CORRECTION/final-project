Rules of Badminton

Learn the fundamentals of the game

Understanding the Rules

Badminton is a fast-paced sport that combines speed, precision, and strategy. While the basic objective is simple—hit the shuttlecock over the net and into your opponent's court—the official rules help keep matches fair and competitive.

Learning the scoring system, serving rules, and common faults is the first step toward becoming a better player.

Key Rules Every Player Should Know

Hover over each card to learn how badminton matches are scored and officiated.

🏆 21 Points

Games are played to 21 points.

21 Point System

Every rally awards a point. The first player or team to reach 21 points wins the game, provided they are ahead by at least two points. Most matches are played as best of three games.

⚖️ Win by 2

You must win by a two-point margin.

Why Win by 2?

If the score becomes 20-20, play continues until one player leads by two points. For example, 22-20 or 25-23. This prevents games from ending on a single lucky point.

🎯 Serving Rules

Every rally begins with a serve.

How to Serve

The shuttle must be struck below the server's waist and travel diagonally into the opponent's service box. If the serve lands outside the correct area, the opponent wins the rally and scores a point.

🚫 Common Faults

Faults immediately give away the rally.

Examples of Faults

Hitting the shuttle out of bounds, touching the net with your racket or body, serving incorrectly, striking the shuttle twice, or allowing it to hit the ground on your side all result in losing the rally.

Understanding the Court

A badminton court contains different zones used for singles and doubles matches. Understanding these areas helps players serve correctly and position themselves effectively.

  • Singles Court: Uses the inner side lines.
  • Doubles Court: Uses the full court width.
  • Service Areas: Determines where serves must land.
